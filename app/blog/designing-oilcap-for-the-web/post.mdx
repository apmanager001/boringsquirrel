# The score rule is the real personality

Oilcap is not interesting because pipes exist on a grid. It is interesting because every placement has a cost and every unnecessary piece drags the score backward.

That means the browser version should avoid turning into a loose sandbox. It should keep the part that makes the old design feel tense:

- route fast
- waste little
- stay readable under pressure

## What should survive the transition

The SVG pipe set in `/public/pipe` is already enough to establish the language of the game. The next step is not more decoration. It is interaction quality:

- drag and drop that feels precise
- a board that reads clearly at a glance
- score feedback that teaches efficiency instead of guessing

<Callout title="North star">
If the player can win while playing messily, the remake has probably lost the thing that made the original memorable.
</Callout>

## Why the landing page matters now

Even before the full interaction ships, the Oilcap page can explain:

- how pieces affect score
- why the game is different from a generic pipe puzzle
- what kind of leaderboard it will reward later

That context helps players understand the promise before a single move is made.
